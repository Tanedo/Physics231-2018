\subsection{A proto-Green's Function} 

Suppose that you need to find the vector $\vec x$, an element of $\mathbbm{R}^3$, given a relation
\begin{align}
	A \vec{x} = \vec{y}_0 \ ,
\end{align}
where $\vec{y}_0$ is known, but $A$ is not known. Instead, you are given the following information about the action of the inverse transform on a particular basis:
\begin{align}
	A^{-1}\ket{1} &= c\ket{1} - s\ket{2}
	&
	A^{-1}\ket{2} &= s\ket{2} + c\ket{1}
	&
	A^{-1}\ket{3} &= \ket{3} \ .
\end{align}
If $\vec y_0 = \sum y^i\ket{i}$ and you know the components $(y^1, y^2, y^3)$ in this basis, what are the components of $\vec x$, $(x^1, x^2, x^3)$, in this basis?
